Day 4 - Task Entity and Designing the Brand

Olaoluwa Oke| 28 February 2024

This afternoon, I sat at my desk trying to figure out why my TaskRequest DTO wasn’t being recognized. I had named it TaskCreateRequest in the actual code. Minor mistake, but it stopped me cold and genuinely made me pause my music



Most of today went into building the Task entity. You know , following the same Interface Metaphor idea, i wanted to name it Chores , more homely , but i actually ironically have a trouble with typing that quickly So we need to be able to arrange tasks or make tasks , or even delete tasks i.e "mop the floor" , the satate of the task , Who did a specific tasjk , you know , these details Tasks are the reason the shared space stays sane — or doesn’t.

I gave each task a name, a description, a due date, and some logic around recurrence and completion. More importantly, it can be assigned to someone — though I had to remind myself that not every task starts off with an owner. So assignedTo needed to be nullable. Took me a second to realize that.



I added the usual DTOs: TaskCreateRequest to receive stuff, TaskResponse to send stuff back, TaskUpdateRequest to edit a task. Basic in structure, a task handed over, and a task reported done.

At one point, I tried parsing a string into an Instant for the due date and blanked. I knew it was something like Instant.parse(...) but I just wasn’t sure if that was enough. Spoiler: it was. But it took asking for help to confirm.



I also wrote a repository method to find tasks by name. And then I realized — what even is a unique task name? Every room on the planet has a “Take out trash” or “Clean bathroom” on repeat. So I’m rethinking that approach. Maybe search by room ID, maybe by both name and who it’s assigned to.
